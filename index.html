Dear Dr. Ronnie,

I am writing to express my interest in the PhD position in software engineering at the University of Calgary.

My passion lies in building intelligent systems that not only solve problems but also understand the people who use them. This belief has guided much of my work as a ML/Software Engineer over the past seven years. Every system I have built has reinforced my conviction that the future of impactful solutions lies in learning systems that can logically represent, adapt to, and even anticipate human needs.

My academic journey has been a continuous exploration of how intelligent systems can learn, adapt, and defend themselves against uncertainty. I have a master's degree in Big Data Technologies, and focused my dissertation on exploring adversarial robustness in ML using ESRGAN. That experience did more than strengthen my technical foundation; it sparked a genuine fascination with generative modeling. I became deeply interested in how models could learn rich, transferable representations from data while remaining secure and trustworthy.

Professional Journey

My professional journey has centered on building intelligent systems that improve healthcare outcomes. My current role focuses on designing intelligent systems that not only process large volumes of data efficiently but also generate actionable predictive insights for decision-making. Working at the intersection of ML and software engineering has highlighted the limitations of conventional machine learning models in capturing complex, context-rich human behaviors. This experience deepened my interest in representation learning and system design, and in developing machine learning systems that reason beyond static inputs and predefined rules.

As my academic and professional experiences converged, my research interests evolved toward the intersection of software systems and human–AI interaction. I am motivated by the challenge of building AI systems that can logically interpret both code semantics and human intent. I am especially interested in exploring how LLMs can enhance code understanding, support modular software reuse, and enable automatic feature extraction through multimodal semantic similarity. I believe this applied perspective will enrich diversity of thought.

This opportunity will be an invaluable stepping stone toward achieving this goal, and I am confident that my background positions me well to contribute to the Institute's research community while benefiting immensely from its mentorship and training.

Thank you for your consideration. I look forward to the possibility of contributing to this research.
